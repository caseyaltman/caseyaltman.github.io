What sets you apart from other structure brokers?

A sophisticated understanding of the settlement process, especially in my home state of New York. So many of my competitors are completely unfamiliar with the application of New York’s varied laws which impact on different categories of settlements. Wrongful Death settlements are treated differently than Personal Injury settlements, which are treated differently than Medical Malpractice settlements, which are further complicated by the New York Medical Indemnity Fund. Understanding the nuances of how each case is calculated for settlement, judgment or distribution to an estate is essential to creating a quality structured settlement quote.

Why make these settlement calculators available online?

I want to provide my clients with accessible tools that will help resolve cases. Knowledge is power and having the ability to calculate quickly, from your computer or your phone, a possible settlement outcome will absolutely help achieve positive settlement results.

How can calculators achieve “positive settlement results?

By assisting in a factual and realistic discussion of damages. The calculators create very precise settlement breakdowns, either by inputting a total desired settlement to see the breakdown, or by inputting settlement components to arrive at a total settlement amount. Each calculator is specific to a body of law governing a claim. For example, a Medical Malpractice claim can be calculated under the NY Medical Indemnity Fund and even with consideration given to a derivative claim allocation to a child’s parent. That detailed breakdown allows for a more precise understanding of a plaintiff’s net recovery which in turn allows for a precise allocation for a structured settlement’s creation.

Similarly, a Wrongful Death claim resulting from either Personal Injury or Medical Malpractice must consider how the case precedents, Matter of Kaiser or Matter of Acquafreda, will allocate settlement proceeds amongst the decedent’s survivors. And that settlement discussion should be based on an understanding of the Toledo case which governs the pre judgment interest owed and discounting of a Wrongful Death verdict in New York under the CPLR.

I want to empower my clients with enabling tools so they can quickly and easily view these calculations and negotiate a settlement.

Understanding Qualified Assignments:
The essential contract that protects the defendants or insurer’s interests when assigning its obligation to make future periodic payments. The assignment transfers full responsibility for all future payments to an independent third party. And when done correctly removes from the defendant all future responsibility to the claimant. This NSSTA handout offers a concise explanation of how defendants and insurers protect themselves with Qualified Assignments.

Information Alerts For Medical Indemnity Fund (MIF) Settlement Calculators

No Disbursements
Attorney Fees are calculated without any deduction for disbursements. The actual cost of the settlement is comprised of the non-MIF damages and the attorney fee allocated to the MIF. (Attorney fees are paid pursuant to NY Judiciary Law §474-a.)

Derivative Claim Cost is calculated by deducting from the Total Settlement the amount of the parent’s share of the settlement. Attorney fees are calculated pursuant to Judiciary Law §474- a. The MIF calculation is adjusted to reflect the reduced Total Settlement attributable to the child’s claim.

Disbursements Calculated on Total Settlement
Disbursements are deducted from the total agreed upon settlement after which the attorney fees are calculated on the total settlement and then allocated to their respective MIF and non-MIF percentages. The disbursements are paid from the actual cost of the settlement, which is comprised of the non-MIF damages and the attorney fee allocated to the MIF. (Attorney fees are paid pursuant to NY Judiciary Law §474-a.)

Derivative Claim Cost is calculated by deducting from the Total Settlement the amount of the parent’s share as well as the pro rata share of the disbursements attributable to the parent’s share of the settlement. Attorney fees are calculated pursuant to Judiciary Law §474-a. The MIF calculation is adjusted to reflect the reduced Total Settlement and Disbursements attributable to the child’s claim.

Disbursements Calculated Pro Rata
Disbursements are apportioned and then subtracted from the respective MIF and non-MIF allocations in order to arrive at the attorney fee for each allocation. The total disbursements are paid from the actual cost of the settlement, which is comprised of the non-MIF damages and the attorney fee allocated to the MIF. (Attorney fees are paid pursuant to NY Judiciary Law §474-a.)

Derivative Claim Cost is calculated by deducting from the Total Settlement the amount of the parent’s share as well as the pro rata share of the disbursements attributable to the parent’s share of the settlement. Attorney fees are calculated pursuant to Judiciary Law §474-a. The MIF calculation is adjusted to reflect the reduced Total Settlement and Disbursements attributable to the child’s claim.

Disbursements Calculated on Non-MIF Damages
Disbursements are deducted from the non-MIF allocation after which attorney fees are calculated on that allocation. Attorney fees are calculated on the MIF allocation without any deduction for disbursements. Disbursements are paid from the actual cost of the settlement, which is comprised of the non-MIF damages and the attorney fee allocated to the MIF. (Attorney fees are paid pursuant to NY Judiciary Law § 474-a.)

Derivative Claim Cost is calculated by deducting from the Total Settlement the amount of the parent’s share as well as the pro rata share of the disbursements attributable to the parent’s share of the settlement. Attorney fees are calculated pursuant to Judiciary Law §474-a. The MIF calculation is adjusted to reflect the reduced Total Settlement and Disbursements attributable to the child’s claim.

Information Alerts For personal injury (PI) Settlement Calculators

Calculate for Settlement Breakdown
Attorney fees are calculated using 33.3333% after subtracting disbursements from the total settlement. Attorney fees can be calculated on the gross settlement pursuant to the 2014 Orders of the 1st, 2nd, 3rd, and 4th Appellate Divisions.
Upfront cash will decrease proportionally to the structured settlement premium allocation entered.

Calculate for Total Settlement
Attorney fees are calculated using 33.3333% after subtracting disbursements from the total settlement. The addition or subtraction of disbursements will not affect the attorney fees when calculating those fees based on a net settlement.

Information Alerts For wrongful death (NY) Settlement Calculators

Wrongful Death – medical malpractice
Settlement is calculated pursuant to Matter of Kaiser 198 Misc 58 (1950), the default setting, or Matter of Acquafredda, 189 A.D.2d 504, 596 N.Y.S.2d 839). After the calculation of attorney fees, the fees, disbursements, liens, expenses (e.g. funeral) and any administrator fee is deducted from the gross settlement. Any allocation for conscious pain and suffering is then made pursuant to EPT 4-1.1 (a) (1). The net distributions for the wrongful death damages and any conscious pain and suffering damages are then tallied and added for each recipient.

The default life expectancy tables are consistent with the Pattern Jury Instructions.
Life expectancy for the decedent and surviving spouse can be customized.

Attorney fees are calculated pursuant to NY Judiciary Law §474-a.

Wrongful Death – Personal Injury

Settlement is calculated pursuant to Matter of Kaiser 198 Misc 58 (1950), the default setting, or Matter of Acquafredda, 189 A.D.2d 504, 596 N.Y.S.2d 839). After the calculation of attorney fees, either based on the gross or net settlement figure, the attorney fees, disbursements, liens, expenses (e.g. funeral) and any administrator fee is deducted from the gross settlement. Any allocation for conscious pain and suffering is then made pursuant to EPT 4-1.1 (a) (1). The net distributions for the wrongful death damages and any conscious pain and suffering damages are then tallied and added for each recipient. Attorney Fees are calculated by multiplying 33.3333% by the net settlement (after subtracting disbursements).

Attorney fees can be calculated on the gross settlement pursuant to the 2014 Orders of the 1st, 2nd, 3rd, and 4th Appellate Divisions.

The default life expectancy tables are consistent with the Pattern Jury Instructions.
Life expectancy for the decedent and surviving spouse can be customized.
